How we've worked

Three sprints of challenges

Shaking things up became a format: cross-team sprints, fresh constraints, AI in every hand. More ideas in three sprints than in the year before.

What we learned

Brilliant for ideation
Not so for execution

Sixteen challenges in, the pattern was clear — and the pace was wearing the team down.

Where it works
Research & ideation
Cross-team teams, fresh constraints, low stakes. The challenge format is our idea engine — and it stays.
Where it doesn't (yet)
Shipping & scaling
Execution wants depth, ownership, and continuity. The sprint format keeps pulling us back to the surface.

The framework

A bar for every new bet

Good ideas are everywhere. A+ ideas are rare — so every new bet has to clear the same nine questions before we commit.

The bar

Nine questions every bet must answer

01
What is it, and who is it for, in one plain sentence?
02
Can it become a billion-dollar product / hit $100M+ ARR?
03
Why now? What just changed that opens the window?
04
How is it 10X better or completely different from the competition?
05
What's the moat that stops others from copying it?
06
What's the distribution and the clear path to users?
07
Why will it win? What's the thesis of victory?
08
Why are we the best team to build this?
09
Once it's live, what's the signal that proves it's an A+ product worth going all in on?
The first bet through the bar
See what people are building.

Plenty of ideas were pitched — many failed the bar. That's the process working. One cleared every question. We're putting real resources behind it.
