The bar

We want conviction

Backed by reasoning — not a sales pitch. The best cases are specific, honest about what's unknown, and have stress-tested themselves. A great pitch makes the risks more visible, not less. "Don't know yet" is a legitimate answer — it tells us where the risk lives.

The shared language

Nine questions every bet must answer

01
Why now? What just changed that opens the window?
02
What is it, and who is it for — in one plain sentence?
03
Can it become a billion-dollar product / hit $100M+ ARR?
04
How is it 10X better or different in kind from the competition?
05
Why will it win? What's the thesis of victory?
06
What's the moat that stops others from copying it?
07
What's the distribution — the clear path to users?
08
Why are we the best team to build this?
09
What's the signal that proves it's an A+ product?

The goal isn't to check every box — it's to think clearly about whether this is worth building.
